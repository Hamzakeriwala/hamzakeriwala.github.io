About

My name is Junaid Patel and I'm 21 years old. I study Computer Science at the University of Toronto, specializing in the Software Engineering stream. Being a co-op student, I worked as a Junior Web Developer in the law firm Osler, Hoskin & Harcourt LLP for 8 months in 2014. Then, I worked as a Web Developer again in the Department of Historical Studies at UofT. This position was under Dr. Shafique Virani, who is a distinguished professor at the University.

My ambition to become a programmer began in high school when I took Computer Science courses in grade 11 as well as grade 12. By developing simple Java programs, I found programming very intriguing and I slowly began having an affinity towards programming. This is when I decided to pursue a career in Computer Science and, from that point on, I've been working on projects in my own personal time as well as in University. From mobile apps to web pages, I'm always thinking of new ideas that could benefit me or be of interest to others. My goal is to move up the "career ladder" and to grow in terms of skills.

ZoteroExtended

  • Client: Natalie Rothman, Associate Professor of History
  • Date: November 2015
  • Service: Mozilla Plugin, Javascript
by The SQL Injectors - Junaid, Nadeem, Minsoo, Andres and Alex This project was part of the Introduction to Software engineering (CSCC01) course. We were put into a team of 5 people and had to develop a plugin for an existing software called Zotero. This plugin was based on requirements provided by a client, a professor from our university (UofT). As part of the project, we followed the AGILE software development method by creating burn down charts based on user stories and having regular meetings, etc. Eventually, we ended up developing a fine plugin that takes care of batch-editing of tags and being able to modify custom output styles. The following is our read me page for this project, from Github.  

Installation

Simply install ZoteroEXT.xpi addon file to Zotero standalone or firefox.

You may also choose to build it yourself:

Manually:
  1. Clone repo
  2. zip the ‘chrome’ folder, chrome.manifest, and ‘install.rdf’
  3. change the .zip extension to ‘.xpi’
Or using our script:
Windows: (from a command prompt) generate_xpi.bat Linux/Mac: sh generate_xpi.sh

Features

Intro video (Please Click to watch!):
  • Batch add, remove, rename, or merge multiple tags in one go!
  • Download, edit, and install user made CSL styles with ease using our built in powerful csl-editor!
